Question
Explain how rising energy prices can cause inflation.
Select the answer you think would get the most marks, then identify the flaws in the others.
A Answer A Likely low marks
Rising energy prices increase costs for businesses and households. This reduces consumer spending and causes demand-pull inflation as people have less money to spend on goods and services.
Potential issues
→Incorrect type of inflation — this is cost-push, not demand-pull
→Weak chain of reasoning
→Limited analysis
I think this is best
B Answer B Some marks
When energy prices rise, firms face higher production costs. They may pass these costs on to consumers through higher prices, causing cost-push inflation. This can lead to a general rise in the price level.
Potential issues
→Limited analysis of how large the impact might be
→No evaluation or counterpoints
→Short-term focus only
I think this is best
C Answer C Best answer
Rising energy prices increase firms' costs of production (e.g. transport, raw materials). Firms pass these higher costs onto consumers, causing cost-push inflation and a leftward shift of the short-run aggregate supply curve (SRAS₁ to SRAS₂).
Why this is strongest
✓Clear economic chain
✓Correct diagram reference
✓Analysis of impact
→Could be developed further with evaluation
I think this is best
